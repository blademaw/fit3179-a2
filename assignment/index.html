Every year, Melbournians rent their living spaces to guests through Airbnb,
and can earn an average of $11,600 AUD annually for a single room. The following
visualisation looks at data compiled in 2018 and analyzes Airbnbs
across the local government areas (LGAs) of Victoria.

What makes up the average Airbnb in Victoria?

Layouts & floor plans

Airbnbs rented out to customers must have at least one full bedroom — in Victoria, Airbnbs have 1.5 bedrooms on average. Most Airbnbs also house at least one bathroom, but just 27% of listings have more than one. Airbnbs can also offer additional amenities to renters and advertise them on their listing. The graphic below displays the most common amenities for Victorian Airbnbs.

Most common amenities in Airbnbs

Rental types & amenities

With over half of Airbnb rentals being apartments, the majority of hosts preferred to rent their entire living area to the customer. Victorians listing their house were also more likely to only let a single room, with 56% of house listings being for private rooms, in comparison to only 24% for apartment listings.


The most common amenity for both apartments and houses was a kitchen — but amenities were often catered to the property type: apartments offered more gyms and pools, while houses ranked higher in step-free access and private entrances.
